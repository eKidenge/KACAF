1. Introduction

Kenya Agroforestry & Climate Action Foundation ("KACAF", "we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, process, and disclose your personal information when you:

  • Visit our website (kacaf.org) or any of our affiliated platforms
  • Register as a member or create an account
  • Participate in our programs, events, or training sessions
  • Make donations or financial contributions
  • Subscribe to our newsletters or communications
  • Volunteer with us or apply for positions
  • Interact with us on social media
  • Contact us for support or inquiries

By using our services, you trust us with your information. We take this responsibility seriously and have implemented comprehensive measures to protect your privacy in accordance with the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, and applicable international privacy laws.

This Privacy Policy is incorporated into our Terms and Conditions. Please read both documents carefully.

2. Key Definitions

Term Definition
Personal Data Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
Sensitive Personal Data Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health status, or biometric data. We collect minimal sensitive data only with explicit consent.
Processing Any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, or disclosure.
Data Controller KACAF determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
Data Processor Third parties who process data on behalf of KACAF (e.g., payment processors, email service providers).
Consent Freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous indication of agreement to processing.

5. How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

Membership Management

Creating and managing your account, processing membership applications, verifying eligibility, communicating membership benefits.

Donation Processing

Processing donations, issuing tax receipts, maintaining donor records, sending thank-you communications, managing recurring donations.

Event Coordination

Registering for events, sending confirmations, managing attendance, coordinating logistics, post-event follow-up.

Program Implementation

Enrolling in programs, tracking participation, measuring impact, reporting to funders, improving program design.

Communications

Sending newsletters, program updates, event invitations, fundraising appeals, responding to inquiries, personalized communications based on interests.

Analytics & Improvement

Analyzing website usage, understanding community needs, evaluating program effectiveness, conducting surveys and research.

Legal Compliance

Maintaining records as required by law, responding to legal requests, preventing fraud, ensuring data protection compliance.

Advocacy & Awareness

Sharing impact stories, promoting climate action, raising awareness (with explicit consent for photos/stories).

6. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019 and GDPR for EU residents, we process your personal data based on the following legal grounds:

Legal Basis Description Examples
Consent You have given clear consent for us to process your personal data for a specific purpose. Marketing communications, photo/video use, sharing impact stories
Contract Processing is necessary for a contract you have with us, or because you have asked us to take specific steps before entering into a contract. Membership agreements, event registration, donation commitments
Legal Obligation Processing is necessary for us to comply with the law. Tax reporting, fraud prevention, regulatory compliance
Vital Interests Processing is necessary to protect someone's life. Emergency contact information at events
Public Interest Processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest. Environmental conservation activities, community development programs
Legitimate Interests Processing is necessary for our legitimate interests or the legitimate interests of a third party, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights and interests. Program improvement, analytics, fraud prevention, donor communications

Where we rely on legitimate interests, we have conducted a legitimate interests assessment to balance our interests against your rights. You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests.

7. Information Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

1. Service Providers & Partners

We engage trusted third parties to perform functions on our behalf, including:

  • Payment processors: Safaricom (M-Pesa), banks, PayPal (for donation processing)
  • Email service providers: Mailchimp, SendGrid (for newsletters and communications)
  • Cloud service providers: For data storage and hosting
  • Analytics providers: Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel
  • Event management platforms: For registration and coordination
  • Consultants and advisors: For program evaluation and reporting

All service providers are contractually bound to protect your data and use it only for the specified purposes.

2. Partner Organizations

We collaborate with partner organizations to implement programs. When necessary for program delivery, we may share limited information with:

  • Government agencies (e.g., Ministry of Environment, county governments)
  • Funding organizations and donors (aggregate, non-identifiable data for reporting)
  • Research institutions (with ethics approval and anonymization)
  • Community-based organizations implementing joint programs

We will obtain your consent before sharing identifiable information with partners unless required for program implementation where you have already enrolled.

3. Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., court orders, law enforcement requests).

4. Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your personal information may be transferred. We will notify you of any such change and outline your choices.

5. With Your Consent

We may share your information for any other purpose disclosed to you and with your explicit consent (e.g., sharing your success story with media).

Commitment: We never sell your personal information to third parties for marketing or any other purposes.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

Data Category Retention Period Justification
Membership records Duration of membership + 5 years Legal and audit requirements
Donation records 7 years (Kenya tax law) Tax reporting and audit compliance
Event registration 3 years Program evaluation and reporting
Website analytics 26 months Analysis and improvement
Newsletter subscriptions Until unsubscribed + 1 year Communication preferences
Contact form inquiries 2 years Reference and follow-up
Employment applications 1 year (unsuccessful applicants) Future opportunities and compliance
Program participation data 10 years (aggregated) Long-term impact assessment

When we no longer need your personal information, we securely delete or anonymize it. In some circumstances, we may anonymize your personal information so that it can no longer be associated with you, in which case we may use such information without further notice to you.

9. Data Security

We implement comprehensive technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction:

Technical Measures
  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmission
  • Encrypted databases for sensitive information
  • Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Secure access controls and authentication
  • Regular software updates and patch management
  • Automated backups with encryption
Organizational Measures
  • Staff training on data protection
  • Confidentiality agreements for all staff and volunteers
  • Access controls based on need-to-know principle
  • Data protection policies and procedures
  • Incident response plan
  • Regular privacy impact assessments
  • Vetting of third-party service providers

Despite our safeguards, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by law.

10. Your Rights & Choices

Under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019 and applicable international laws, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right to be Informed

You have the right to be informed about the collection and use of your personal data (fulfilled through this Privacy Policy).

Right of Access

You can request a copy of the personal information we hold about you, free of charge.

Right to Rectification

You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete information.

Right to Erasure

You can request deletion of your personal information where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing.

Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask us to suspend the processing of your personal data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability

You can request your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.

Right to Object

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.

Rights related to Automated Decision-Making

You can request human intervention in decisions based solely on automated processing.

How to Exercise Your Rights:

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information in the Contact section below. We will respond to your request within 30 days (as required by Kenyan law).

Communication Preferences:

You can manage your communication preferences at any time:

  • Click "unsubscribe" in any marketing email
  • Update your account settings
  • Contact us directly to update preferences

Marketing Cookies Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag: track conversions, retargeting, audience building 90 days - 2 years Browser settings / Platform settings
Social Media Cookies Enable sharing on social platforms, track interactions Varies by platform Platform settings

12. Children's Privacy

KACAF is committed to protecting children's privacy. Our programs often involve working with children and youth, and we have special safeguards in place:

  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent.
  • For children aged 13-18, we require parental/guardian consent for participation in programs and collection of personal data.
  • When we collect information about children (for program participation), we:
    • Obtain verifiable parental consent
    • Collect only information necessary for program participation
    • Use information only for the specified purpose
    • Do not share children's information with third parties (except as required for program delivery with consent)
    • Provide parents with access to their child's information and the right to request deletion
  • Photos and videos of children in our programs are only used with explicit parental consent.
  • We implement enhanced security measures for children's data.

If you believe we may have collected information from a child without appropriate consent, please contact us immediately at privacy@kacaf.org.

13. International Data Transfers

KACAF is based in Kenya, but we may transfer your personal information to service providers located in other countries. When we transfer data internationally, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Adequacy decisions: For transfers to countries deemed adequate by the Kenya Data Protection Commissioner or EU Commission.
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: We use approved contractual clauses to protect data transferred to countries without adequacy decisions.
  • Binding Corporate Rules: For transfers within our partner network where applicable.
  • Explicit consent: Where appropriate, we obtain your explicit consent for international transfers.

Our primary service providers are located in:

  • Kenya (local data hosting, M-Pesa)
  • United States (email services, analytics, cloud hosting)
  • European Union (certain processing activities)

By using our services, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries that may have different data protection laws than your jurisdiction.

14. Third-Party Links

Our website and communications may contain links to third-party websites, plug-ins, and applications (e.g., Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, partner organizations). Clicking on those links may allow third parties to collect or share data about you.

We do not control these third-party websites and are not responsible for their privacy practices. When you leave our website, we encourage you to read the privacy policy of every website you visit.

This Privacy Policy applies solely to information collected by KACAF.

15. Changes to Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or operational needs. We will notify you of material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website with a new effective date
  • Sending an email notification to registered users (for significant changes)

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information. Your continued use of our services after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

18. Complaints Procedure

If you believe we have not handled your personal information properly or have violated your privacy rights, you have the right to lodge a complaint.

Internal Resolution Process:
  1. Contact our Data Protection Officer directly at dpo@kacaf.org
  2. We will acknowledge your complaint within 48 hours
  3. We will investigate and provide a substantive response within 30 days
  4. If you are unsatisfied with our response, you can escalate to our Executive Director at director@kacaf.org
