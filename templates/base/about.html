Our Mission

To empower communities in West Pokot County through sustainable agroforestry, biodiversity conservation, and climate resilience initiatives that restore ecosystems, improve livelihoods, and build a greener future for generations to come.

Our Core Values

The principles that guide every action we take

Community First

We believe in the power of communities. Every project is designed with and for the people, ensuring local ownership and sustainable impact that lasts generations.

Environmental Stewardship

We are guardians of the earth. Our commitment to protecting and restoring ecosystems guides every decision, ensuring a healthy planet for future generations.

Integrity & Transparency

We operate with honesty and openness. From financial management to program implementation, we maintain the highest standards of accountability.

Innovation

We embrace new ideas and technologies. From climate-smart agriculture techniques to digital monitoring systems, we continuously innovate to maximize impact.

Inclusivity

Everyone has a role to play. We actively include women, youth, and marginalized groups in all our programs, ensuring equitable benefits for all.

Sustainability

We think long-term. Every project is designed to be self-sustaining, creating lasting change that continues to benefit communities for years to come.

Our Journey

From humble beginnings to regional impact

2015

The Beginning

KACAF was founded by a group of environmentalists and community leaders in West Pokot, starting with just 50 farmers and a vision to restore degraded lands through agroforestry.

2017

First Major Milestone

Successfully planted 5,000 trees and trained 200 farmers in sustainable agriculture practices. Recognized by the County Government for outstanding community work.

2019

Expansion Phase

Expanded operations to 5 new locations, launched the Women in Agroforestry program, and established partnerships with international organizations.

2021

Climate Action Initiative

Launched comprehensive climate resilience programs, including water harvesting, drought-resistant crops, and carbon sequestration projects.

2023

Digital Transformation

Implemented digital monitoring systems, launched member portal, and reached 15,000 trees planted milestone with 2,500+ active members.

2025

Today & Beyond

Operating 12 active programs, 25 staff members, partnerships with 15 organizations, and ambitious goals for the next decade of impact.
